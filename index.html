The Elements of The Table



What is the Periodic Table

The periodic table is a tabular array of chemical elements arranged by atomic number, beginning with hydrogen and ending with oganesson. An element's atomic number
refers to the number of protons in its nucleus. The periodic table allows scientists to easily refer to information about an element, such as its atomic mass and chemical symbol.
The periodic table's design also enables scientists to identify patterns in element attributes such as electronegativity, ionisation energy, and atomic radius.
